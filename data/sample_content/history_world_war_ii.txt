World War II Summary

World War II (1939–1945) was a global conflict involving most of the world's nations, divided into two opposing military alliances: the Allies and the Axis powers. It is considered one of the deadliest wars in history, with an estimated 70–85 million fatalities.

Key Events:
- The war began on September 1, 1939, when Nazi Germany, led by Adolf Hitler, invaded Poland. This prompted France and the United Kingdom to declare war on Germany.
- In 1941, the United States entered the war after Japan attacked Pearl Harbor on December 7.
- The war in Europe ended on May 8, 1945 (V-E Day), when Germany surrendered. The war in the Pacific ended on September 2, 1945 (V-J Day), after the U.S. dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki.

Major Figures:
- Adolf Hitler: Leader of Nazi Germany and a central figure in the Axis powers.
- Winston Churchill: Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, a key leader of the Allies.
- Franklin D. Roosevelt: President of the United States during most of the war.
- Joseph Stalin: Leader of the Soviet Union, which joined the Allies after Germany invaded in 1941.

The war led to significant geopolitical changes, including the establishment of the United Nations and the beginning of the Cold War between the U.S. and the Soviet Union.
